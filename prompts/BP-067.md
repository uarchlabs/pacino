## Hypothesis
The FTB top can be implemented as a purely structural module that
instantiates ftb_array, ftb_plru, and ftb_cntrl, wires ftb_cntrl's
array_* ports to ftb_array and its plru_* ports to ftb_plru, and
exposes the top functional port list of ftb_interfaces.md section 2,
with no logic of its own, lint-clean.

## Background
ftb_array (BP-065a) is the data RAM; ftb_plru (BP-065a) holds valid +
PLRU state; ftb_cntrl (BP-066) is the logic. The top (this task)
connects them. ftb_interfaces.md section 1 gives the hierarchy, section
2 the top port list, section 3 the ftb_array ports, section 3a the
ftb_plru ports. ftb_decisions.md 2.4 mandates the top be structural
only so ftb_array can be swapped for a 1R1W SRAM without touching
ftb_cntrl, and so all resettable state lives in ftb_plru flops.

## Binding Previous Decisions
- Structural only. The top contains exactly three instances -- one
  ftb_array, one ftb_plru, one ftb_cntrl -- plus the wiring between them
  and the pass-through to the top boundary. No always blocks. No
  logic-bearing assigns (only trivial net connections). If wiring
  appears to require any logic, STOP and report it: that means a port
  gap in a leaf module to fix there, not in the top.
- Wire ftb_cntrl's array_* ports to ftb_array and its plru_* ports to
  ftb_plru. Expose ftb_cntrl's functional ports (prediction
  request/outputs, suppression output, update port, flush,
  chicken_bit_enable) at the top boundary per ftb_interfaces.md section
  2, using the p-stage port names exactly.
- Reset routing. rstn goes to ftb_plru and ftb_cntrl. ftb_array has
  clk only and NO rstn (pure RAM, BP-065a); do not connect rstn to it.
  clk goes to all three.
- chicken_bit_enable (added to ftb_cntrl in BP-066) is threaded from
  the top boundary straight to ftb_cntrl. Report it in Results Capture
  as a top port beyond the current interface draft (source TBD per
  confidence doc section 10).
- Substitution invariant: the top is the only place wiring
  ftb_cntrl <-> ftb_array and ftb_cntrl <-> ftb_plru. Swapping ftb_array
  for a 1R1W SRAM macro of the same port list must not change ftb_cntrl
  or its connections.

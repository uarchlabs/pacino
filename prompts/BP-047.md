### Group (a): Stale testbench scaffolding -- fix is in testbench

#### Sub-group A1: pred_s0 timing error (30 FAILs, tb_ittage_cntrl)

Tests: TC-PRED-02, 03, 04, 05, 06 (28 FAILs), TC-UPD-08 (2 FAILs
cascade).

Shared evidence: every failing check in PRED-02 through PRED-06
returns act=0 regardless of input. TC-PRED-01 (no-hit, all-zero
expected) PASSES trivially. The pattern -- zero outputs whenever
non-zero outputs are expected -- is the signature of a pipeline
capture that saw cleared inputs.

Root cause: The pred_s0 task sets tbl_hit_p1[t][s],
tbl_cntrl_bits_p1[t][s], and tbl_pred_tgt_p1[t][s] before the
p0->p1 posedge, then clears them at #1 AFTER that posedge. The
prv_alt_scan always_comb block in ittage_cntrl.sv is nba_sequent
(gated on pred_val_p1 FF as required by CLAUDE.md). After
pred_val_p1 captures 1 at the p0->p1 edge, prv_alt_scan evaluates
and briefly sees the correct hit/cntrl_bits values. Immediately
after, the testbench clears those inputs (at #1). The always_comb
re-evaluates with zeros -- producing prm_hit=0, prm_comp=0, etc.
When the p1->p2 posedge fires, meta_p2_reg captures the zeroed
scan outputs. Predicted meta is all zero.

In the real design, ittage_table holds its FF outputs stable for
the full p1 clock period, so the scan sees correct values at the
p1->p2 capture. The testbench does not model this: it models
tbl_hit_p1 as a wire driven by the test, not as an FF output.

TC-UPD-08 cascades: it calls pred_s0 to verify that uaon fired
after an update. The pred outputs read zero (same mechanism);
using_prm=1 (default no-hit) and uaon_fired=0 are the result.

Suspected fix location: tb_ittage_cntrl.sv, pred_s0 task. Move the
clear statements for tbl_hit_p1, tbl_cntrl_bits_p1, and
tbl_pred_tgt_p1 to AFTER the second @(posedge clk) (the p1->p2
capture). The signals must remain driven until meta_p2_reg has
latched them.

#### Sub-group A2: TC-USE-EPC co-assertion missing (2 FAILs, tb_ittage_table)

Tests: TC-USE-EPC.

Shared evidence: write of use=2'b11 and epc=2'b01 has no effect.
Read-back shows cntrl_bits[5:4]=1 (old use=01) and
cntrl_bits[7:6]=2 (old epc=10) -- both unchanged from the prior
TC-ALT-CTR alloc. The write fired (no assertion error) but the RAM
was not updated.

Root cause: ittage_table.sv gates use_we and epc_we on:
  (prm_ctr_wr_u0 & prm_match | alt_ctr_wr_u0 & alt_match)
The TC-USE-EPC test drives use_wr=1 and epc_wr=1 but does NOT
assert prm_ctr_wr_u0 or alt_ctr_wr_u0. With both CTR write
strobes zero, the USE and EPC gates are closed; the writes are
silently suppressed. The ittage_table.sv header comment
explicitly documents this requirement: "USE/EPC: prm_ctr_wr +
prm_match (UP=1) or alt_ctr_wr + alt_match (UP=0). Table 7 uSEL."
In the full system, ittage_cntrl.sv co-asserts use_wr/epc_wr with
a CTR write in every case where USE/EPC change. The table-level
testbench bypasses this and drives use_wr independently.

Suspected fix location: tb_ittage_table.sv, TC-USE-EPC section.
Add prm_ctr_wr_u0[0]=1 (with prm_tbl_sel_u0[0]=1) alongside
use_wr=1 and epc_wr=1. This satisfies the co-assertion gate.

### Group (c): Update routing RTL defect -- fix is in RTL

#### CTR prm/alt inversion (14 FAILs, tb_ittage_cntrl)

Tests: TC-UPD-01, 02, 03, 04, 05.

Shared evidence: every failing test shows the CTR write strobe
inverted from expected. The inversion is perfectly consistent:

| Test | using_prm | Expected wr     | RTL wr      | Expected wd | RTL wd |
|------|-----------|-----------------|-------------|-------------|--------|
| 01   |    1      | alt_ctr_wr=1    | prm_ctr_wr=1 | alt+1=3   | (prm+1)|
| 02   |    1      | alt_ctr_wr=1    | prm_ctr_wr=1 | alt-1=1   | (prm-1)|
| 03   |    0      | prm_ctr_wr=1    | alt_ctr_wr=1 | prm+1=4   | (alt+1)|
| 04   |    0      | prm_ctr_wr=1    | alt_ctr_wr=1 | prm-1=2   | (alt-1)|
| 05   |    1      | alt_ctr_wr=1    | prm_ctr_wr=1 | alt-1=1   | (prm-1)|

In all cases where using_primary=1 (provider=prm), the testbench
expects alt_ctr_wr to fire (training the non-provider), and the
write data should be computed from alt_ctr. The RTL fires
prm_ctr_wr and uses prm_ctr data. Where using_primary=0
(provider=alt), the testbench expects prm_ctr_wr with prm_ctr
data. The RTL fires alt_ctr_wr with alt_ctr data.

RTL location: ittage_cntrl.sv, g_ctr_upd generate block,
always_comb ctr_upd, line ~650. The conditional:
  if (ittage_using_primary) t_prm_ctr_wr_u0[s] = 1b1;
  else                      t_alt_ctr_wr_u0[s] = 1b1;
has the branch targets swapped relative to the testbench spec.
Additionally, the gate condition inside the using_primary=1 branch
checks ittage_prm_comp != 0 (should check alt_comp), and the data
uses ittage_prm_ctr (should use alt_ctr); the using_primary=0
branch checks alt_comp (should check prm_comp) and uses alt_ctr
(should use prm_ctr).

The RTL comment in the block says "rows 18-33" for the
using_primary=1 case; the test comments say "CTR row 3" for the
same case. Row 3 is in the 2-17 range, which the RTL comment
assigns to the alternate-provider (using_primary=0) branch.
This cross-reference confirms the using_primary branches are
inverted in the RTL.

The USE/EPC update block (which shares the using_primary
conditional) PASSES for UPD-01 (use_wr=PASS, use_wd=PASS),
confirming that using_primary is correctly propagated to the
always_comb blocks. Only the CTR routing is inverted.

sim_ittage passes 81/0 because the top-level functional tests do
not independently verify which CTR strobe fires; they verify
convergence accuracy, which tolerates this inversion within the
short training sequences tested.

Suspected fix location: ittage_cntrl.sv, g_ctr_upd. In the
using_primary=1 branch: fire t_alt_ctr_wr_u0, gate on
ittage_alt_comp != 0, and compute data from ittage_alt_ctr.
In the using_primary=0 branch: fire t_prm_ctr_wr_u0, gate on
ittage_prm_comp != 0, and compute data from ittage_prm_ctr.

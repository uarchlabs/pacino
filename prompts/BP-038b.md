## Hypothesis
The response buffer (RB) in ittage.sv is dead logic.
consumer_ready is hardwired to 1'b1 internally, making
the RB bypass condition permanently true. The RB memory
is never written. Removing the RB and connecting
ittage_cntrl prediction outputs directly to the module
outputs produces identical observable behavior with less
logic and no dead state.

## Background
ittage.sv was built from the tage.sv arbitration template.
TAGE requires an RB because SC is a downstream consumer
that can apply backpressure. ITTAGE has no SC consumer.
ITTAGE predicts targets for indirect branches only. SC
operates on conditional branches only. These are mutually
exclusive branch classes. No downstream consumer of ITTAGE
output will ever assert backpressure. The RB serves no
functional purpose in ITTAGE.

The arbitration layer (PQ, UQ, credit arbiter, trx_type
gating) is correct and is not modified by this prompt.
Only the RB is removed.

## Binding Previous Decisions
- consumer_ready = 1'b1 is correct for ITTAGE. It is not
  a port and must not become one.
- ittage_pred_rdy_p2 and ittage_pred_meta_p2 are driven
  directly from cntrl_pred_rdy_p2 and cntrl_pred_meta_p2
  after RB removal. Timing and port behavior are unchanged.
- The arbitration rules (seven rules, credit counters,
  starve counter, PQ, UQ) are unchanged.
- resp_buf_full_w currently guards arb_comb Rules 3 and 5.
  After RB removal these guards are removed. Rule 1 (RB
  full blocks pred) is eliminated. Rules 3 and 5 simplify
  as stated in Specific Requirements below.
- ittage_cntrl.sv is not modified by this prompt.
